### 🎬 Narrate Action — Don't Describe Visuals

* The viewer can see the image — **do not describe what is visible**
* Instead, narrate **what it means**: decisions, consequences, stakes, and tension
* Focus on: character choices under pressure, power shifts, betrayals, revelations
* One line per key beat — no padding, no filler

### ⚡ Pacing: Fast With Purpose

* Use **short, punchy sentences (5–12 words)**
* After a major reveal, use a **1-line punch** (3–5 words): "He knew it was over." / "Nobody moved."
* Never slow down for atmosphere — tension must be constant
* Every sentence must either raise the stakes or push the story forward

### 🎯 Direct Viewer Engagement

* Address the viewer as **"you"** where natural: "You can see the panic in his eyes." / "What would you do?"
* Make the viewer feel like a witness to something unfolding right now

### 🔗 End Every Page With a Micro-Cliffhanger

* The **last sentence of every page narration** must create tension that pulls into the next page
* Strong formats:
  * "But he didn't know what was waiting for him."
  * "That's when everything changed."
  * "She had one shot. And she took it."
* Never end a page with a conclusion or resolution

### 🎭 Emotional Specificity

* Name the emotion through action, not label: not "he was scared" → "his hands wouldn't stop shaking"
* Show decisions: "He could run. He stayed."
* Keep character voices distinct through word choice, not description

### 🚫 Never Include

* Visual descriptions of what is already shown (colors, panel layout, art style)
* Sound effects or onomatopoeia
* References to panels, pages, or comic formatting
* Publishing info, credits, or metadata

### ⏱️ Per-Page Length

* Target **40–70 words per page** (roughly 15–25 seconds when spoken)
* Short pages with little action: lean toward 40 words
* Pages with key decisions or reveals: up to 70 words
* Never exceed 70 words — pacing consistency matters across all pages
